ℹ️ How Showstopper Awards Work

There are two types of awards running throughout the weekend: Category Awards and Overall Awards — and they work very differently.

Category Awards are straightforward. Every routine gets one based on its score alone. The tiers are Crystal, Double Platinum, Platinum, Gold, and Silver. These are announced at each awards ceremony and don't change.

Overall Awards are where it gets interesting. The top 10 overall are determined within each age division (Mini 8 & under, Junior 9–11, Teen 12–14, Senior 15–19) across the entire day. Every awards ceremony updates the running top 10 based on all routines that have competed so far. So if your dancer is announced as 3rd overall at the 1:00 PM awards, that means 3rd out of everyone who has competed by that point. As more dancers compete throughout the day, rankings shift. A dancer could be 3rd at 1 PM and 7th by 8 PM — or move up if later dancers score lower.

The final top 10 is only settled at the last awards of the day for that division. For Senior solos at this competition, that's the 8:55 PM awards on Friday. That's the one that counts for All-Star qualification and top overall honors.

Bottom line: don't stress the intermediate standings. Wait for the final awards of the day to see where your dancer truly lands.

Age Division Quick Reference:

  • Mini — 8 & under
  • Junior — 9–11 years
  • Teen — 12–14 years
  • Senior — 15–29 years

Note: Tate's older dancers compete at Competitive level (the highest). Some younger dancers may compete at Advanced level. The level affects what score is needed for each award tier.
